At the corner of FM 535 and Wolf Lane in Cedar Creek, something historic is taking shape. EdgeConneX, one of the largest data center developers in the world, is building AUS02, a $440 million, 578,000 square foot facility set to come online in June 2026. And that is only the beginning.

AUS02 is the first of four buildings planned for a 140 acre campus, a full build-out that represents a potential $1.44 billion investment in Bastrop County. When complete, the campus will deliver 96 megawatts of capacity in its first phase alone, powering the cloud services and artificial intelligence workloads that have become the backbone of the modern economy.

This is the kind of generational opportunity that most rural counties never get. Before the noise of social media takes over the conversation, Bastrop County residents deserve the facts.

## Why This Investment Matters for Every Family in Bastrop County

Economic developers have a term for what happens when a billion dollar commercial project lands in a rural county. They call it the Net Community Benefit, and it compares what a project pays into the tax base against what it costs the county in new services.

Compare the math on two different growth paths:

A 1,000 home subdivision at roughly $300 million in combined property value adds an estimated 1,500 new students to the Bastrop or Smithville ISDs, sharply increases daily traffic, puts new strain on emergency services, and pressures the county's water infrastructure.

A $1 billion data center campus, by contrast, adds zero new students, generates minimal daily vehicle traffic, operates its own on-site security and fire suppression systems, and typically funds its own utility upgrades.

The comparison is not close. A data center campus is one of the most tax-efficient commercial assets any county can host. It pays enormous property tax revenue while requiring almost none of the public services that residential growth demands.

That surplus flows in three directions, all of which benefit local families.

### 1. Fully Funded Schools Without Raising Your Taxes

Rural independent school districts in Texas have historically operated on tight budgets. A hyperscale data center campus injects millions of dollars directly into the local ISD through property taxes, and because no new students arrive with the facility, 100 percent of that revenue goes toward teacher pay, modernized classrooms, and better technology for the kids already here.

Think about that. New revenue, no new cost pressure on the classroom.

### 2. Stronger Police, Fire, and EMS

Bastrop County's population has been growing fast. Emergency services have been stretched to keep up. A large commercial taxpayer like EdgeConneX provides the liquidity counties need to fund new fire engines, EMS stations, and sheriff's deputies without pushing the bill onto homeowners through rising property tax rates.

### 3. Real Tax Relief for Homeowners

This is the part the opposition never mentions. When a county's budget runs short because of residential growth, the bill lands on individual property owners. A billion dollar commercial asset on the tax rolls shoulders a massive share of the county's total burden, which takes pressure off the homeowners who have lived here for decades.

Without major commercial taxpayers, the entire weight of county infrastructure falls on working families. That is the trajectory we avoid when we welcome investment like this.

## The 6x Jobs Multiplier

Critics sometimes point out that a running data center does not employ thousands of workers inside the walls. That is true, and it also misses the point.

Research on data center economic impact has documented a 6x multiplier effect: for every direct job inside the facility, roughly six indirect and induced jobs are supported across construction, electrical and mechanical trades, maintenance contracts, catering, transportation, local suppliers, and professional services.

The AUS02 construction phase alone puts hundreds of skilled tradespeople to work in our county right now. Long term operations support electricians, HVAC technicians, security personnel, landscapers, and the dozens of local businesses that serve them. These are the kinds of high-wage, future-proof careers that let the next generation build a life in Bastrop County without having to move to Austin, Houston, or Dallas.

## Setting the Record Straight on the Misinformation

Whenever a major project is announced, a predictable wave of misinformation follows. Most of it sounds scary at first, and most of it falls apart the moment you check the numbers. Here are the two claims you are hearing most often, along with what the data actually shows.

### Myth 1: "Data centers will drain our water."

Total freshwater consumption by all U.S. data centers combined was less than 0.5 percent of America's total freshwater use in 2023. For context, American cities lose an estimated 3 trillion gallons of water every year through leaky municipal pipes, roughly 15 times more water than every data center in the country consumes combined. Agricultural irrigation uses more than 100 times what data centers use.

And modern facilities, including the closed-loop designs preferred by developers like EdgeConneX, are engineered to use dramatically less water than older generations. Many use air cooling, liquid immersion cooling, or reclaimed wastewater, keeping freshwater draw near zero.

The idea that a Cedar Creek campus is going to dry up Bastrop County wells is not supported by any credible data.

### Myth 2: "Data centers will crash the grid and raise our electric bills."

This one ignores how modern data centers actually work. Hyperscale operators increasingly build their own power generation on site, solar arrays, battery storage systems, natural gas turbines, and in some cases small modular reactors. That is called behind-the-meter power, and it reduces the burden on the local grid rather than adding to it.

Even better, large battery storage systems installed at these facilities can send power back to the grid during peak demand or severe weather, acting as a stabilizer during exactly the kind of Texas summer heat events that stress ERCOT. Far from being a drain, a well-designed data center campus is a grid asset.

## A Piece of a Bigger Picture

The EdgeConneX investment does not exist in isolation. Bastrop County has quietly become one of the most important technology corridors in the state, home to Tesla's manufacturing and headquarters, xAI, Starlink, The Boring Company, and Neuralink. A $43 million grant is also extending gigabit fiber across the county, delivering fast broadband to every resident.

This is a virtuous cycle. Serious capital attracts more serious capital. Bastrop County is positioning itself to capture a generation of growth while preserving the historic character and natural beauty that make this such a special place to live.

## The Bottom Line for Bastrop County

AUS02 is more than a building. It is a commitment. EdgeConneX is putting $440 million into Cedar Creek right now, with the potential for another billion to follow. Every dollar of that investment strengthens our schools, our emergency services, and the property tax burden carried by local families.

The opposition is loud. The math is louder. When you cut through the noise, the case for this project is not close.

Bastrop County's tomorrow is being built today, and the Cedar Creek data center campus is one of the most important pieces of it.
